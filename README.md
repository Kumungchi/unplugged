> **Unplugged exists to help people and institutions understand what AI can do well, where its limits matter, and how to use it without losing human judgment, real relationships, or healthy boundaries.**

<br>

## ❖ Why Unplugged Exists

Artificial intelligence is becoming part of everyday life. People use AI to think, write, learn, plan, reflect, and sometimes to cope with loneliness, stress, emotional discomfort, uncertainty, or relational pain. 

More and more often, AI is also used in roles that feel deeply personal: as a listener, a confidant, a therapist-like figure, a friend-like presence, a romantic companion, or even an intimate substitute. In schools and workplaces, it is becoming a practical tool for productivity, communication, and decision support.

**That creates both opportunity and risk.**

AI can be useful, but it can also be misunderstood, overtrusted, anthropomorphized, or used in ways that quietly replace something deeply human: *real support, real accountability, real friendship, real intimacy, and real care*.

Unplugged was created to respond to that gap. We focus especially on situations where AI is used as:
- An emotional support substitute
- A pseudo-therapist or conversational tool
- A friend, companion, or intimate stand-in
- A default response to loneliness, uncertainty, overload, or relational difficulty

**Our goal is not to create panic around AI. Our goal is to make AI use more clear, informed, human-centered, and sustainable.**

<br>

## ❖ Mission & Vision

### 🎯 Mission
Unplugged helps people and organizations use AI safely, ethically, and with healthy boundaries, so that AI supports human abilities, relationships, and decision-making rather than replacing them.

### 👁️ Vision
We aim to become a trusted Czech and, over time, European initiative for:
- **AI literacy** and healthy AI use
- **Ethical AI boundaries**
- **Education** about AI in relationships, friendships, and therapy-like contexts
- **Practical public-interest guidance** at the intersection of technology, psychology, and society

<br>

## ❖ Core Themes

Unplugged is especially concerned with the following questions:

| Theme | Core Question |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Relationships & Intimacy** | *What happens when AI starts taking on the role of a partner, emotional confidant, or romantic substitute?* |
| **Friendships & Loneliness** | *What happens when AI becomes easier than people, and begins replacing real friendship, social effort, or human reciprocity?* |
| **Therapy-like Use** | *What happens when people turn to AI as if it were a therapist or psychologically safe authority, even though it does not carry responsibility, embodiment, clinical judgment, or real care?* |
| **Literacy & Boundaries** | *How can people and institutions understand AI clearly enough to use it well without sliding into dependence, confusion, or overtrust?* |
| **Responsible Use** | *How should schools, universities, and workplaces respond to growing AI use in a way that is informed, ethical, practical, and psychologically realistic?* |

<br>

## ❖ Core Principles

**1. AI is a tool, not a relationship.**  
> AI can assist, structure, and inform. It does not care, attach, reciprocate, or take responsibility in the way humans do.

**2. Healthy AI use is a real skill.**  
> Using AI well is not just technical. It also requires judgment, boundaries, context, and self-awareness.

**3. Human connection still matters.**  
> Not every intimate-feeling interaction is a real relationship. Not every responsive conversation is friendship. Not every reflective exchange is therapy. Unplugged encourages movement back toward real-world support, reflection, and human contact.

**4. Education before panic.**  
> We do not approach this topic through fearmongering. We aim for clarity, literacy, prevention, and practical guidance.

**5. Ethics must stay practical.**  
> Unplugged is interested in ethics that help people act better in real contexts — not only abstract debates.

<br>

## ❖ What Unplugged is NOT

Unplugged is **not**:
✖️ A therapy service or crisis intervention line  
✖️ An AI companion product or romantic/emotional chatbot  
✖️ A replacement for friendship, intimacy, or professional mental health care  
✖️ A platform designed to maximize emotional dependency on AI  

<br>

## ❖ Current Project Direction

The project is currently being developed as a **mobile-friendly educational web platform** with future potential to grow into a broader institute / public-interest initiative.

Early directions include:
- A public-facing website and short **“reality check”** for AI use.
- Educational materials about healthy AI boundaries and therapy-like use.
- Workshops for schools, universities, and organizations.
- A longer-term institutional and policy-facing layer.

As AI adoption increases across emotionally significant areas of life, questions around overreliance, emotional dependency, AI companions, and responsible deployment are becoming more urgent. Unplugged is being built as a practical response to that moment.
